Rule

MCQ


  1. Clarity and Conciseness: Ensure that each question and its corresponding options are clear and concise. Ambiguity can lead to confusion and misinterpretation.
  2. Single Best Answer: Each MCQ should ideally have only one correct answer. Avoid questions where more than one option may seem correct or where none of the options are correct.
  3. Avoid Negative Wording: Try to avoid negatively worded questions (e.g., "Which of the following is NOT..."). They can confuse test-takers and increase the chance of misinterpretation.
  4. Avoid Absolute Terms: Be cautious with using absolute terms like "always," "never," "completely," etc., in both questions and options, as there may be exceptions that invalidate the statement.
  5. Randomization: If possible, randomize the order of options to prevent test-takers from relying solely on position-based cues.
  6. Avoid Trivial Questions: Ensure that the questions assess meaningful understanding of the topic and avoid including questions that are too easy or too obscure.
  7. Review and Revision: Always review and revise your MCQs before using them in assessments. Get feedback from peers or subject matter experts to ensure accuracy and fairness.


Coding Challenges


  1. Clear Problem Statement: Provide a clear and concise problem statement that outlines the task or problem the participant needs to solve. Ambiguity can lead to confusion and frustration.
  2. Define Input and Output: Clearly specify the input format, constraints, and expected output for the problem. Understanding the input-output behavior helps participants formulate their solutions effectively.
  3. Example Cases: Include example cases to illustrate how the solution should behave under different inputs. Example cases provide clarity and serve as a guide for participants.
  4. Test Cases: Provide a set of test cases to validate the correctness and efficiency of the participants' solutions. Test cases should cover a range of scenarios, including edge cases and corner cases.
  5. Time and Space Complexity: Encourage participants to consider the time and space complexity of their solutions. Emphasize the importance of writing efficient code that performs well under different input sizes.
  6. Feedback Mechanism: Offer a feedback mechanism where participants can test their solutions against provided test cases and receive immediate feedback on correctness and performance.
  7. Continuous Improvement: Gather feedback from participants and iterate on the challenges based on their experiences and suggestions. Continuous improvement ensures that the challenges remain relevant and engaging over time.
